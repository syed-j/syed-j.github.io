// Overview

The Challenge

USF Athletics lacked a unified view of its fan base. Ticket sales, merchandise purchases, and survey feedback lived in separate silos with no way to understand who fans are, what drives them, or how to convert casual attendees into loyal supporters.

Students loved the game experience (rating it 4.6/5), but pre-game friction — particularly venue entry traffic, which scored only 3.03/5 — was hindering repeat attendance. The core question: how do you turn a first-time visitor into a season ticket holder?

// Data Foundation

Three Data Sources, One Truth

The project began by ingesting and cleaning data from three distinct platforms, then joining them on a shared geographic key (city) to create a unified fan dataset.

// Process

Project Phases

The project followed an Agile framework across two 4-week sprints, with weekly deliverables and iterative stakeholder feedback.

WEEKS 1–3 · RESEARCH & PERSONAS

Fan Persona Development

Analyzed Florida DMA demographics (Esri) and USF fan survey data (Vision Insights) to create data-backed personas. Identified four distinct fan segments based on spending behavior, attendance frequency, geography, and engagement channel preferences.

WEEKS 4–6 · DATA PREPARATION

Data Cleaning & Integration

Imported all three datasets into SQLite using DBeaver. Cleaned invalid entries, resolved duplicate Order IDs, standardized date formats, and performed a JOIN on city to link merchandise buyers with ticket holders. Created the unified dataset powering all downstream analysis.

WEEKS 7–8 · AGILE PLANNING

Sprint Planning & KPI Definition

Defined SMART goals: +8% attendance growth, +5% season-ticket renewal, +10% merchandise conversion. Built an Agile project plan with two 4-week sprints, clear acceptance criteria, and risk mitigation strategies for data gaps and privacy compliance.

WEEKS 9–12 · SPRINT 1

Dashboard MVP & KPI Visualization

Built an interactive dashboard with three core KPIs: Average Spend Per Fan Segment, Attendee Retention Funnel (42,500 → 15,725 returners → 6,800 die-hards → 2,975 season members), and Geographic Penetration across 10+ states. Integrated an OpenAI-powered "Generate Persona Insights" button.

WEEKS 13–16 · SPRINT 2

AI Chatbot & Final Presentation

Prototyped a Student Gameday Assistant Chatbot designed for GoUSFBulls.com — answering parking questions, recommending arrival times, delivering student promo codes, and collecting post-game NPS feedback. Compared Microsoft Azure vs. Satisfi Labs frameworks and recommended a hybrid approach.

// Analytics

Key Performance Indicators

Three KPIs were defined to measure fan engagement across financial value, retention, and geographic reach.

KPI 1

Average Spend Per Fan Segment

Quantifies the financial value of different fan segments to identify high-value groups and growth opportunities. Die-Hard fans average $225 vs. $75 for Casual — a 3x revenue gap driving targeted strategy.

(Total Ticket Price + Total Merch Price) / Unique Fans in Segment

KPI 2

Attendee Retention Funnel

Tracks the journey from first-time attendee to season ticket member. Of 42,500 total 2023 attendees, 37% returned for a 2nd game, 16% became die-hard (3+ games), and 7% converted to season tickets.

(First-Time Attendees Who Return) / (Total First-Time Attendees)

KPI 3

Geographic Penetration

Measures marketing effectiveness across regions and identifies "cold spots." Georgia led out-of-state fans with 1,800+ combined ticket + merch buyers, followed by NC and SC.

(Zip Codes with Sales) / (Total Target Market Zip Codes)
// AI Integration

AI-Powered Engagement

The project incorporated AI at two levels: auto-generated dashboard insights and a proposed conversational chatbot for students.

📊 Dashboard AI Insights

Integrated an OpenAI API-powered "Generate Persona Insights" button that produces real-time narrative summaries of KPI trends, marketing recommendations, and anomaly callouts — letting non-technical staff extract actionable intelligence without writing queries.

💬 Student Gameday Chatbot

Designed a conversational AI assistant for GoUSFBulls.com that answers arrival/parking questions in real-time, recommends optimal arrival windows, delivers student promo codes, and collects post-game NPS feedback. Projected impact: -20% traffic complaints, +10% repeat attendance, +15% promo conversion.

🔧 Framework Comparison

Evaluated Microsoft Azure (Copilot Studio + Cognitive Search + Fabric) vs. Satisfi Labs (Custom LLM + Ticketing APIs). Recommended a hybrid approach: Azure backend for scalable data management, Satisfi frontend for sports-specific conversational AI.
